// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your project images—including the Rock Island Maintenance Complex in Malaga, WA—are almost certainly published without JSON-LD schema linking them to geographic coordinates, project type, or client sector, making them invisible as structured data assets to Google's image indexing pipeline. Every untagged photo of that Chelan County PUD build is a dead file that costs you inbound discovery from facilities directors running exactly that search. Fix the EXIF geotag, alt-text, and schema simultaneously or the images do zero structural work.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Public agency procurement coordinators—the exact people authorizing RFQ responses for school districts and transit authorities like Ben Franklin Transit—leave your site without initiating contact because your written claims of "fiscal stewardship" and "resilience" have zero photographic substantiation. There are no documented construction-phase images, no stakeholder meeting captures, no before/after sequencing of the Rock Island campus transformation that proves your process rather than just asserting it. A facilities director managing public funds needs visual evidence of your operational competence, not mission language.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Larger Seattle-area firms named alongside you in the Puget Sound Business Journal ranking are deploying dedicated project documentation assets—aerial photography, phased construction sequences, and occupant-in-use imagery—tied directly to their education and operations sector pages, creating visual portfolios that function as pre-qualification evidence. Your operations and community practice area pages carry no project-specific photography that differentiates your work from a text description any firm could publish. That gap costs you shortlist placement on public sector RFQs where visual project history is evaluated before a firm is even contacted.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured documentation shoot at Rock Island and the Ben Franklin Transit facility that captures pre-occupancy, occupancy-day, and operational-use phases as distinct asset categories—not a single walkthrough gallery. Each image file must be deployed with embedded EXIF location data, descriptive alt-text referencing project name, county, and facility type, and JSON-LD schema on the page marking it as an architectural project with geographic and sector attributes. That protocol converts your completed project inventory into a search-discoverable, procurement-ready evidence library that works 24 hours a day without a salesperson.
